Dashboard Story
Audience: Leadership Data: Sales Performance Dashboard — 4,200 orders across 2024–2025

1. Executive Summary
The business generated total sales of 217,017,652 and total profit of 33,306,313 across 4,200 orders over two years (2024–2025). The overall profit margin is 15.3% and the return rate is 4.5%. Sales grew slightly from 2024 to 2025 — from 106,241,026 to 110,776,626 — showing a positive but modest upward trend.

The dashboard tells a clear story: Technology is driving almost all the profit, South is the strongest region, and Furniture is dragging down overall margins. High discounts are consistently linked to lower profit and even losses.

2. What is Performing Well
Technology dominates profit Technology accounts for 153,895,268 in sales — more than Furniture and Office Supplies combined — and delivers an 18.2% profit margin. The top three most profitable sub-categories are all Technology: Copiers (7,310,055), Accessories (7,186,863), and Phones (7,097,291).

South region leads in sales and profit The South region generated the highest sales (64,693,707) and highest profit (9,987,912) of all four regions. It outperforms the next best region (North) by about 10 million in sales.

Organic channel is the most valuable Organic campaign channel brings in the highest sales (88,783,981) and profit (13,444,457) — more than double any other channel. This suggests that brand reputation and word of mouth are strong drivers of revenue.

Sales are growing year on year Sales increased from 106,241,026 in 2024 to 110,776,626 in 2025. Profit also grew from 16,472,754 to 16,833,559. The growth is steady even if not dramatic.

3. What is Underperforming
Furniture has very low profit margins Despite 51,641,016 in sales, Furniture only delivers a 6.9% profit margin — less than half of Technology's 18.2%. Sub-categories like Paper (318,707), Binders (325,668), and Storage (342,231) are the least profitable across the entire business.

Office Supplies contributes very little overall Office Supplies generates only 11,481,368 in sales — the smallest of the three categories — with a 14.9% margin. While the margin is reasonable, the volume is too low to make a big impact.

West and East regions are the weakest West (48,908,980 sales) and East (48,859,164 sales) both trail South and North significantly. Their profit figures are also the lowest at 7,404,073 and 7,599,443 respectively.

Home Office segment has the highest return rate Home Office customers return items at a rate of 5.7% — noticeably higher than Consumer (3.9%) and Corporate (4.0%). This is worth investigating as returns reduce effective revenue.

4. What Risks are Visible
High discounts are hurting profit There is a clear negative relationship between discount rate and profit (correlation of −0.266). Orders with a discount above 20% average only 2,915 in profit, compared to 9,821 for orders with 20% or less discount. This is more than a 3x difference.

288 orders are loss-making Out of 4,200 orders, 288 have a negative profit — meaning the business is actually losing money on those transactions. These are likely concentrated in high-discount orders and Furniture sub-categories.

Furniture return rate is the highest Furniture has a 7.7% return rate — more than double Technology's 3.0%. Returns in a high-cost, low-margin category like Furniture are particularly damaging.

5. What Opportunities are Visible
Grow Technology sales further Technology already delivers the best margins. Prioritising Technology in marketing spend and inventory would likely improve overall profitability without needing to fix underperforming categories.

Review discount strategy The data clearly shows that deep discounts are not generating enough additional sales to justify the profit loss. A more controlled discount policy — particularly keeping discounts below 20% — could significantly improve overall margins.

Invest more in Organic and Email channels Organic (13,444,457 profit) and Email (5,364,558 profit) are the top two channels by profit. These are relatively low-cost channels that are already delivering strong returns. Increasing focus here makes commercial sense.

Investigate Furniture profitability Furniture generates decent sales volume but very thin margins. Leadership should assess whether pricing, supplier costs, or discount policies in Furniture can be improved — or whether to reduce focus on this category.

6. Recommended Business Actions
Reduce discounts above 20% — the data shows these consistently produce low or negative profit. A discount cap policy would protect margins.

Focus marketing on Technology products — highest margin, highest profit, and lowest return rate. Any extra investment here is likely to pay off.

Investigate Home Office returns — at 5.7% return rate this segment needs attention. Understanding why Home Office customers return more could reduce unnecessary revenue loss.

Double down on Organic and Email channels — these deliver the most profit for the business. Consider shifting budget away from Paid (6,053,064 profit) and Referral (3,336,084 profit) which underperform relative to their cost.

Review Furniture pricing and costs — 6.9% margin on a category generating 51 million in sales is leaving a lot of money on the table. Even a 2–3% margin improvement would add over 1 million in profit.

7. Limitations of the Dashboard
The data covers only 2024 and 2025 — two years is a short period to confirm long-term trends or seasonal patterns reliably.
Customer-level data is not fully used in this dashboard. We can see segment performance but not individual customer behaviour or loyalty.
The return flag only tells us whether an order was returned — it does not tell us why. Without return reasons, we cannot fully diagnose the Furniture or Home Office return problem.
Campaign channel data shows which channel an order came from but does not include the cost of each channel, so true ROI cannot be calculated from this data alone.
Profit figures do not include overhead costs like rent, salaries, or logistics — so the margins shown here are gross margins, not net profit.
8. Suggested Next Analysis
Break down loss-making orders by sub-category and discount level to find exactly where money is being lost
Analyse whether specific states or cities are driving the South region's strong performance — and whether that success can be replicated elsewhere
Look at repeat purchase behaviour by customer segment to understand whether Consumer, Corporate, or Home Office customers are more loyal
Track monthly sales trends within each year to identify any seasonal patterns that could inform stock and staffing decisions
